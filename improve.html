Basic Tips that Helped Me Improve

Priority of Play

The concept of priority of play is crucial in football (soccer) as it serves as a guiding principle for effective decision-making on the field. Essentially, priority of play refers to the optimal direction in which to advance the ball. The primary focus should always be on playing the ball forward, as this represents the shortest route to the goal and maximizes the team's chances of scoring. However, defenders will often position themselves to block this path.

In such cases, many beginners mistakenly opt to pass the ball backward when faced with defensive pressure. Instead, the next best option is to play the ball diagonally forward, which allows players to maintain proximity to the ball while If both the forward and diagonal forward options are unavailable, the team should consider playing the ball to the sides. This lateral movement forces defenders to shift, potentially creating gaps in their formation.

If all three options—forward, diagonal forward, and sideways—are covered, the team can then play the ball diagonally backward. This strategy not only keeps the opponents moving but also provides the attacking team with the opportunity to reset their formation while complicating the defenders' efforts.

Only when all these avenues are exhausted should the team resort to playing the ball back, as this can waste the progress made and invite counter-attacks from the opposition.

Speed of Play

The speed of play is another tip that is good to remember to help with your decision makings. Let's face it, we all loathe the "one man" guy, who hogs the ball for his dear life. Yes, that tactic may work, if you are possessed with world-class dribbling talent. However, in the recent years, football philosophy has been adapting and evolving into a more passing emphasized plays. This is simple. A player will never out run the ball, both speed and stamina (enduance).

A general rule of thumb is this: A pass will always outrun a dribble. No matter how fast a player is, they will never outrun a ball that is passed with the right weight and accuracy. Passing the ball is much more efficient, since players won't have to sprint everytime, and have more control with their plays. It's much easier to steer a car going 35mph than a car going 120. The same principle is applied here. A slower smoother distribution will give us advantage to hold control of our plays, give us time to think, and rest our body for when it actually needs a sprint.

Tactical Plays

Tiki-Taka

Tiki-Taka is a style of play in soccer that focuses on short, quick passes and maintaining possession of the ball. The direct translation of "Tiki-Taka" comes from Spanish, where "tiki" refers to the sound of the ball being passed, and "taka" refers to the sound of the ball being received. This tactic was created and popularized by the Spanish national team and FC Barcelona, especially under the management of Pep Guardiola in the late 2000s and early 2010s.

To execute Tiki-Taka, players constantly move to create space and options for each other. They make quick, short passes, often in triangles, to keep the ball moving and to avoid losing possession. The players need to be very skilled at passing and have good awareness of their teammates' positions. This style requires a lot of teamwork and communication.

The benefits of Tiki-Taka include controlling the game by keeping the ball away from the opponent, which reduces their chances to score. It also tires out the opposing team, as they have to chase the ball. By maintaining possession, teams can create opportunities to attack while minimizing risks of counter-attacks from the other side. Overall, Tiki-Taka emphasizes skill, teamwork, and strategic play.

Collective Press

Collective pressing, often referred to simply as "pressing," is a tactic in soccer where a team works together to win back possession of the ball as quickly as possible after losing it. The idea is to apply pressure on the opposing players, especially those who have the ball, to force them into making mistakes or hurried decisions.

In collective pressing, players coordinate their movements to close down space and limit the options available to the opponent. This means that when one player presses an opponent, nearby teammates also move in to support, creating a collective effort to regain possession. The team works as a unit, often trying to trap the opposing players in certain areas of the field, making it harder for them to pass or move the ball effectively.

The benefits of collective pressing include disrupting the opponent's play and creating opportunities to win the ball back in dangerous areas, which can lead to quick scoring chances. It also puts psychological pressure on the opposing team, making them feel rushed and more likely to make errors. Overall, collective pressing is an aggressive and proactive approach that can lead to exciting and dynamic gameplay.
