Basic Rules & Regulations

Yellow Card vs. Red Card

The main difference is the severity of the foul the player did. The two cards are a punishment to the player. A yellow card is typically given out as a warning to the player. 2 yellow cards make up a red. In a red card, once the card is given; the player is ejected from the game. He will not only go back to the bench, but usually go back to the locker rooms, since the player is "physically" not allowed on and near the pitch.

Fun fact: The most player with the most red card is Gerardo Bedoya, who has 46.

Different Types of Kicks

Basic Pass (Ground Ball)

To execute a basic pass (ground ball) in soccer, start by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart for balance, similar to preparing to putt in golf. Picture your kicking foot as a putter, which you'll use to gently push the soccer ball forward. Take a few small steps towards the ball, aligning your body over it, and position your non-kicking foot beside the ball, pointing in the direction you want to pass. Swing your kicking foot back, then push it forward to strike the ball with the inside of your foot. Allow your foot to follow through in the direction of your target, creating a smooth motion that generates a forward spin on the ball, ensuring it rolls smoothly on the ground. Practice this technique with a partner or against a wall, focusing on foot positioning and a controlled push to refine your passing skills.

Lob Pass (Air Ball)

To execute a lob pass (air ball) in soccer, visualize your kicking foot as an iron club used in golf. Start by positioning your body over the ball. As you prepare, imagine chopping your foot down toward the ground to strike the bottom of the ball, creating lift. Swing your kicking foot down and then upward, making contact with the ball to generate a backspin, which helps it stay in the air and land softly. After striking the ball, allow your legs to follow through briefly in the direction of your target, maintaining a smooth motion. Practice this technique with a partner or against a wall, focusing on the downward strike and follow-through to refine your lob passing skills.

Curve Shot

To execute a curve shot in soccer, begin by positioning your body to whip the ball diagonally upward, similar to a sweeping motion. As you prepare to kick, strike the ball with the inside of your foot, ensuring your foot follows through in a banana-like shape. This diagonal rotation creates a curve, allowing the ball to travel along a curved path. Focus on the smoothness of your motion, allowing your legs to follow through naturally to enhance the curve. Practice this technique with a partner or against a goal, concentrating on the angle of your kick and the follow-through to perfect your curve shot. When perfected, this shot is very useful to deceive the goalkeepers, freekicks, and corner kicks.

Power Shot

To execute a power shot in soccer, visualize kicking straight through the ball, as if your foot is driving through its core. Start by positioning yourself directly behind the ball, ensuring your body is balanced and your non-kicking foot is planted beside it, pointing in the direction you want to shoot. As you strike the ball, focus on making solid contact with the laces of your kicking foot, following through powerfully in the direction of your target. This follow-through will help you generate immense power and speed in your shot. Practice this technique by taking shots on goal, concentrating on your foot placement and the force of your follow-through to maximize the effectiveness of your power shot. An accurate power shot is very difficult to defenders & goalkeepers to block.
